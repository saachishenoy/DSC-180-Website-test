Enhancing Historical Understanding with Retrieval Augmented Generation

Project Overview

This project addresses the challenge of navigating the vast amount of information on the internet to retrieve historically relevant and accurate data. By leveraging a Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) framework that combines search algorithms with Large Language Models (LLMs), our model searches, retrieves, and synthesizes data from historical news sources to respond to user queries with nuanced insights.

Methodology

Our methodology involves preprocessing newspaper data, storing it efficiently, and implementing a two-tiered search and retrieval process. Initial methods utilized BERT embeddings and FAISS indices, while improvements led to adopting Sentence Transformers for more efficient processing and Chroma databases for scalable storage. Our final responses are generated by the LLamas LLM, optimized for answering complex historical queries.

Results

Through comparative model analysis and user satisfaction testing, our project demonstrates improvements in the efficiency and accuracy of historical information retrieval. Our refined methodology showed increased user satisfaction, underscoring the effectiveness of our tool in providing detailed and relevant historical insights.

Discussion and Conclusion

This project illustrates the potential of RAG in enhancing historical understanding, with our methodology serving as a valuable tool for researchers and educators. Despite limitations such as potential biases in source material, the project sets a foundation for future innovation in historical information retrieval.
